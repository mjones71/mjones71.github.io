How the College Basketball Preseason Rankings Turned Out

NCAA
Basketball
AP Rankings
Author

Maliik Jones

Published

November 5, 2024

The AP top 25 polls come in each week of the NCAA Basketball season. These are the compiled pollings from sportswriters and broadcasters all around the country. So, just how accurate are these rankings from the start of the season to the end.

I took a look at the stats from the top five basketball teams from the preseason and then compared them to the stats of the final top five teams. Obviously, I’m not going to look at each and every category so I summed up defense into steals, blocks, and rebounds. Then I summed up offense into field goal percentage, three point percentage, and assist to turnover ratio.

Personally, I believe that these are purely guesses and it’s not possible to KNOW who’s going to be a top caliber team. Maybe you can tell if a team will win most of their games, but winning at a high level? College basketball has way too many factors and underdogs to truly know.

Also I wonder what really defines these teams and makes them top five in the country…

While, offensively these teams were pretty similar in stats to the top five teams in the preseason. So, is defense the big factor to build a real top five caliber squad? I guess it really does win games and championships as UConn went on to win it all last season.
