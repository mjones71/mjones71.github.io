Author

Similar to the NCAA Basketball AP rankings, college football has their own top 25 ranked teams. I went and compared the top five squads from the 2023 preseason to the end of the season. Specifically looking at their offensive stats and how they differed. Breaking them down into two main categories of offense: passing and rushing. With sub-categories of passing percentage, passing yards, and passing touchdowns for passing the ball. Then rushing average, rushing yards, and rushing touchdowns for running the ball.

I believe that to stand out and be known as an elite squad you must excel on the offensive end of the ball. College football games tend to have high scoring results and which team can put the ball in the end-zone most efficiently will rise to the top.

pretop5pass <- fball |>
  filter(TeamFull=="Georgia Bulldogs"|
          TeamFull=="Michigan Wolverines"|
          TeamFull=="Ohio State Buckeyes"|
          TeamFull=="Alabama Crimson Tide"|
          TeamFull=="LSU Fighting Tigers"
         )|>
  select(TeamFull, PassingPct, PassingYds, PassingTD) |>
  group_by(TeamFull) |>
  summarize(
    TotalPassPct = sum(PassingPct)/12,
    TotalPassYds = sum(PassingYds),
    TotalPassTD = sum(PassingTD))

pretop5pass |> 
  gt() |> 
  cols_label(
    TeamFull = "Team",
    TotalPassPct = "Total Passing Percentage",
    TotalPassYds = "Total Passing Yards",
    TotalPassTD = "Total Passing Touchdowns"
  ) |>
  tab_header(

The Longhorns did great when it came to the ground game and even though the Huskies didn’t rely on running as much, they clearly found their strong suit in the passing game. When they did run they still were able to average 5 yards per carry which is higher than Alabama’s run game.

The offensive game at a high level is insane, I mean Michigan and Georgia had 80+ passing completion percentage. As well as being able to average around 6 yards per carry on the ground. It’s like these top teams are completely unstoppable on this end of the ball.
